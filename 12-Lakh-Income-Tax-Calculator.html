₹12 Lakh Income Tax Calculator (Updated for Budget 2025-26)

The Indian government has recently announced significant changes to the income tax slabs for 2025-26, bringing major relief to middle-class taxpayers. If you earn ₹12 lakh annually, you might not have to pay any income tax due to new tax slab adjustments and rebates.

New Income Tax Slabs (2025-26)

As per the latest updates, the new income tax slab rates under the new tax regime are:

Income Slab Tax Rate
Up to ₹4 lakh 0% tax (No tax liability)
₹4 lakh - ₹8 lakh 5% tax
₹8 lakh - ₹12 lakh 10% tax
₹12 lakh - ₹16 lakh 15% tax
₹16 lakh - ₹20 lakh 20% tax
₹20 lakh - ₹24 lakh 25% tax
Above ₹24 lakh 30% tax

How Much Tax Do You Pay on a ₹12 Lakh Salary?

Let's calculate the total tax payable for a ₹12 lakh annual income under the new tax regime:

Step-by-Step Tax Calculation

Income Slabs Breakdown

  • First ₹4 lakh → 0% tax → ₹0 tax
  • Next ₹4 lakh (₹4L - ₹8L) → 5% tax → ₹20,000 tax
  • Next ₹4 lakh (₹8L - ₹12L) → 10% tax → ₹40,000 tax

Total Tax Before Rebate = ₹60,000

Tax Rebate Under Section 87A

The government has increased the tax rebate limit to ₹60,000, which means that if your total tax liability is ₹60,000 or less, you get a full rebate.

Since our calculated tax (₹60,000) is within the rebate limit, the final tax payable is ₹0.

Thus, if you earn ₹12 lakh per year, you don’t have to pay any income tax in the new tax regime.

How to File Zero Tax Return for ₹12 Lakh Income?

Even if your tax liability is zero, it is important to file your ITR for benefits like loan approvals, visa applications, and maintaining financial records.

Steps to File ITR for ₹12 Lakh Income

  1. Visit the Income Tax e-Filing Portal (https://www.incometax.gov.in)
  2. Login with PAN and Password
  3. Select ITR Form → Choose ITR-1 (Salaried Individuals)
  4. Enter Your Salary & Other Income Details
  5. Verify That Your Taxable Income is ₹12 Lakh
  6. Claim the Rebate (Section 87A) Automatically Applied
  7. Submit & Verify Using Aadhaar OTP

Your ITR will be successfully filed with zero tax payable!

Tips to Maximize Tax Savings

Even though income up to ₹12 lakh is tax-free, here are some additional tax-saving strategies:

  • Invest in 80C Schemes: PPF, ELSS, FD, Life Insurance – Even if you are in the new regime, these investments help in wealth creation.
  • Utilize 80D for Medical Insurance: Claim up to ₹25,000 for health insurance premiums (self/family).
  • HRA Benefits (Old Regime): If you stay in a rented house, consider the old regime for HRA deductions.
  • NPS & EPF Contributions: Employer contributions to EPF/NPS are tax-free and help in retirement planning.
  • Claim Home Loan Benefits (Old Regime): If you have a home loan, interest on housing loan is deductible under Section 24.

Conclusion: Should You Switch to the New Tax Regime?

If you earn ₹12 lakh per year, the new tax regime (2025-26) is a great option since it allows zero tax due to the ₹60,000 rebate under Section 87A. However, if you have significant investments in HRA, 80C, or home loan benefits, you might still want to compare both regimes.

Final Verdict:

  • ✔ New Tax Regime is better for individuals earning up to ₹12 lakh due to full rebate.
  • ✔ Old Tax Regime is better for high-income individuals with multiple tax-saving investments.
